    <h3 class="landmark heading" id="work">Chapter Text</h3>
    <p>
  <span>Phoenix’s back hurts.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>That’s not </span>
  <em>
    <span>new, </span>
  </em>
  <span>not at all, but it does get particularly noticeable whenever he has to lift something. Like now, for instance, when he’s helping Maya move some of her spirit channelling… equipment? Memorabilia? He didn’t really ask for the details. She called him, asked for some help out in Kurain because one of the channeling chambers needed the floor redone and they had to clear the place out, and he can’t say no to his sister. So now he’s here, hoisting a box that sounds like it might be full of beads and metal statues, and his back hurts.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The things he’s carrying, whatever they are, just need to be moved to another building a block or two over. It’s not terribly far, but it still makes that undercurrent of hurt in his limbs flare up.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Stopping in front of the building that seems to be a temporary storeroom, Phoenix can’t help the way he breathes a sigh of relief. Gingerly, but probably still with a little too much force, he sets the box down with a groan, hunched over even without the weight in his arms anymore. It’s annoying when his body gets like this- not just his back, but everything. Some kind of dull ache in his bones that twinges when he moves wrong. Today’s just particularly bad.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>He straightens out with another huff of discomfort, right hand pressed to his spine as a crease settles between his eyebrows. At the very least, he and Maya are almost done here.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Speaking of Maya- Phoenix turns to face her, somewhat awkwardly with the way he’s trying to un-fuck his back, eyebrows ever so slightly raised. She’s been oddly quiet for the past half-hour or so, the cool, open air of Kurain devoid of her usual quips and jabs. It borders on concerning, honestly. Maya Fey is never without a witty comment- or at the very least, an affectionate insult.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The silence practically burns. Before he really thinks about it, he’s opening his mouth to fill it.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I’m getting old, huh?” Phoenix grits out with an awkward half smile in lieu of Maya poking fun at him. The atmosphere is a little… off, without it. More than a little. He doesn’t like it.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Maya, however eager she usually is, doesn’t rise to the bait. She barely responds, actually, standing a few feet away with a crease in her brow and a strange skew to her lips. Something small and anxious blooms in Phoenix’s gut the longer the silence between them stretches on, and he can’t help but cough into his hand, wincing at the way it makes his shoulders complain. Physical labor of the lifting variety has never been his strong suit. Riding a bike doesn’t give you much in the way of upper body strength, and he doesn’t lift weights, so-</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Wait. Shit. Off topic, back to the moment, </span>
  <em>
    <span>focus, Phoenix, focus.</span>
  </em>
</p><p>
  <span>Phoenix wants to say something here. The way Maya is looking at him is a little too intense, edged with something he can’t put a name to that feels the slightest bit like she’s trying to break him open. It’s weird. It’s very weird, strange, </span>
  <em>
    <span>off</span>
  </em>
  <span>- whatever word you want to use, it’s not a part of their usual rappor. He squirms a little, fixed to the spot and suddenly incapable of using his words as the eyes on him give him some kind of ominous once over, before Maya’s expression abruptly softens. His shoulders relax at having that almost-glare off of him- and oh, yeah, okay, moving them down like that hurt a bit. Fuck. Alright.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Just because she isn’t staring him down with the gravitational force of the sun anymore doesn’t mean Maya has stopped </span>
  <em>
    <span>looking. </span>
  </em>
  <span>Her gaze is still fixed on Phoenix, an emotion he can’t identify just behind her eyes, and he can’t help the way he blurts out a nervous “Everything okay?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I should be asking </span>
  <em>
    <span>you </span>
  </em>
  <span>that.” Maya shoots right back, and- wait, what?</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Phoenix blinks, bewildered. Maya somehow seems unphased by the inherent strangeness of the words that just came out of her mouth. Honest to god, he doesn't see what there is to be concerned about here beyond the strange lull in Maya’s own behaviour. Something in his brain stutters to a halt.</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>“...Um. Why?” He manages to fumble out, eyes wide and eyebrows furrowed. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>Maya twitches slightly, like she wasn’t expecting that sort of response, her jaw suddenly set with the kind of determination that so often spells trouble. It makes concern flare in Phoenix’s chest again, before she takes a deep breath- clearly stable, just… preparing to say something? Huh?- and stares him down again.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Nick.” She starts, tone earnest and as stern as he’s ever heard it. “Are you… always in this much pain?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>What.</span>
</p><p>
  <em>
    <span>What.</span>
  </em>
</p><p>
  <span>Phoenix feels his mouth fall open slightly, and he’s certain his expression looks just as baffled as he feels right now.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“...Huh?” Is all that comes out, his thought process ground to a halt and confusion seeping into every part of his body language. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I mean,” Maya starts, like it’s the most obvious thing in the world, “I’ve been thinking, y’know? And you get like this a lot- like, a </span>
  <em>
    <span>lot</span>
  </em>
  <span>- where you scrunch up and get all crabby and keep trying to joke about stuff even though you look tense enough to break in half. Something’s hurting you, right?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Her search for conformation seems more like her reaffirming something she already knows for sure, and Phoenix can’t help the jerky, perplexed nod he gives.</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>“Exactly!” Maya carries on. “And like, you totally know your body better than me, but… Nick, don't you think that maybe hurting as often as you do is a bad thing?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Phoenix feels something in his brain spin out, like a car that’s lost all traction on an icy road. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Well- I, I mean- maybe?” He splutters, moving his hands with nervous energy that threatens to make him burst at the seams, the dull ache in his forearms pulsing sharply for a second when he bends his wrist wrong. “I don’t- Maya, what are you getting at here?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“What I’m </span>
  <em>
    <span>getting at </span>
  </em>
  <span>is that I’m worried about you.” She says, tone matter of fact as her eyebrows furrow slightly. “And you don’t need to help me move things if you’re in pain.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Phoenix stares, slack jawed and unsteady on his feet, until he makes the impulsive decision to sit down, back to the outer walls of the house being used for storage. It’s an awkward adjustment- the movement is too fast and he’s pointedly ignoring the ache in his knees as he crosses his legs on the ground- but Maya seems to get the idea relatively quick, plopping down beside him with that strange look of concern still plastered all over her face. It makes his head spin.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“...I want to help you, though.” Phoenix mumbles, trying his best to start back where they left off. “I don’t know what you’re so worried about.”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>Maya purses her lips, the crease in her brow deepening. The look really doesn’t suit her.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“...Nick. Can you answer something for me?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Phoenix blinks. “Uh. Sure.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“How much pain are you in right now?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“No more than usual.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>Alarm flares behind Maya’s eyes, but there doesn’t seem to be any surprise in the expression. “And… how much is usual?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>A frown crosses Phoenix’s face, lips twisting as he mulls the question over. How do you rate that kind of thing? Scales at the doctor's office never help- he doesn’t know what a one means, is it no pain, or just a little? What qualifies as bad enough to say ten? Is five manageable, or just middle of the road terrible? Adjectives don’t work either, really, because ‘it hurts’ is obvious, but saying it’s awful or excruciating or anything like that feels like an exaggeration. And besides, he’s not even hurting </span>
  <em>
    <span>that </span>
  </em>
  <span>much. It’s a bit worse today than it is most of the time, the muscles in his back twinging like nobody's business and his legs more than a little wobbly from the dull ache in his joints, but that's just because of the work. It happens. It’s normal, and it doesn’t really get to him most days, and-</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Nick.” Maya says, cutting through his thoughts with her gentle tone. “Nick. It shouldn’t take that long to answer.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>He sputters for a moment, words failing him until he blurts out “It’s- it’s not that bad.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You’re in </span>
  <em>
    <span>pain,</span>
  </em>
  <span>” Maya repeats emphatically, and he both loves and hates how persistent his sister is when she puts her mind to things, “that’s bad all on its own. What hurts?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Um. Most things?” He says with a shaky attempt at a smile, hoping it comes off as some sort of supid, poorly executed joke. The veneer falls quickly, though, Phoenix lapsing into a bit of genuine honesty with Maya looking at him the way she is. “My back especially, I guess- like I said, I’m getting old.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“You’re twenty-six.” She shoots back, but it sounds less like a quip and more like an expression of concern. “But- most things? Like… most of you hurts?”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Well. Uh, when you put it that way-”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>“So everything hurts, is what you’re saying.” Maya cuts him off before he can backpedal, that same alarm from earlier plain and slightly grim on her face. “And it feels like that all the time?”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>Phoenix opens his mouth, some kind of deflection resting on the tip of his tongue before he thinks better of it. His shoulders slump, eyes shifting to stare at the ground as he fidgets with the edge of his shirt.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Um. Pretty much, yeah.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Shit.” Maya replies distantly, like she wasn’t expecting Phoenix to concede so easily. “That… that sucks. I’m sorry.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“It’s fine.” He replies on instinct, before an expression of confusion settles onto his face. “But- but everyone feels like that, right? At least a little bit?” </span>
</p><p>
  <span>Maya shakes her head almost apologetically, and Phoenix feels that last shred of denial in his chest crumble to nothing. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>“...Oh.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“Most people only feel pain when they get injured, or put a lot of strain on themselves.” His sister starts to explain, sympathy clear as day in the tone of her voice. “It’s… really not normal to just- to just hurt like that all the time.”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>She pauses, one hand reaching up to touch the magatama around her neck, fingers brushing over the smooth surface. “I’m not in pain right now. I’m a little worn out from all the moving around, but… that’s it. A sore muscle or two tomorrow is the worst that’ll come from this for me.” Maya’s eyes shift to make contact with his as he glances over at her. “It’s not like that for you, though, is it?”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>Phoenix can’t help the strained chuckle that escapes him. “Yeah. It’s not.”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>Maya doesn’t push, sitting next to him in the heavy silence that covers the two of them like a blanket now. For a minute or two, Phoenix doesn’t speak,- can’t speak- his thoughts winding around him so tightly it's hard to breathe.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>It’s not like he hadn’t been expecting this. It was just a whole lot easier to hope that maybe, </span>
  <em>
    <span>maybe, </span>
  </em>
  <span>this was normal. Nevermind that the pain started up in full when he was twenty-one, ignoring the fact that the people around him never seemed to have to take weeks at a time to recover from an active three days, putting aside the way people looked at him just a little funny when he had to lean on things to stay fully upright from the ache in his legs. His head feels like it’s spinning.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I- you really aren’t in pain?” </span>
</p><p>
  <span>Maya shakes her head. “Nope. Not at all.”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>“Not even a little?”</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span><br/>
</span>
  <span>“No pain, Nick. Just a little tired.”</span>
  <span></span><br/>
<span><br/>
</span>
  <span>He wheezes another laugh. “Fuck.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The look on Maya’s face is pure sympathy, the barest hint of her usual mood lifting smile quirking the corners of her mouth. “Yeah. Fuck.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>The ache that permeates every part of his body feels so much more present all of a sudden, his mind tuned into the hurt, and Phoenix almost wants to cry- but Maya’s arms are around him in a heartbeat, and now he’s </span>
  <em>
    <span>definitely </span>
  </em>
  <span>crying, ugly tears of frustration and realization but mostly pain soaking into the shoulder of her acolytes garb. She holds him tightly, and he holds her right back, too-tense shoulders be damned. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>The crying doesn’t last long. Really, it’s a momentary flash of everything just being </span>
  <em>
    <span>too much </span>
  </em>
  <span>that fades away to the usual kind of discomfort days like these bring, even with an added layer of ‘oh fuck, this isn’t normal’ on top. Phoenix pulls back, wiping at his eyes as he sniffles and Maya lets him, even though she looks like she’d really rather be doing more.</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“...I think you need to see a doctor, Nick.” She says softly after a moment, placing one gentle hand on Phoenix’s shoulder. </span>
</p><p>
  <span>God, what would he do without her?</span>
</p><p>
  <span>“I... I think you might be right.”</span>
</p><p>
  <span>And that’s the end of it, for now. The conversation’s reached its natural end point, and Phoenix feels wrung out like a scratchy novelty washcloth. Maya gets it- she always gets it when it matters- and stands up, scooping up the box Phoenix hadn’t managed to get inside. She pauses, flashing him a brief, warm smile, her eyebrows still upturned with worry. And then she carries it through the door, out of his sight for a split second before she hops back out, sitting down next to him in commiserative silence- and despite the low pain humming in almost every part of Phoenix’s body, it’s the lightest he’s felt in a while.</span>
</p>
